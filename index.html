FAQ

How often should I visit the dentist?

It is generally recommended that you visit the dentist every six months for a routine check-up and cleaning. However, your dentist may recommend more frequent visits based on your individual oral health needs.

What can I expect during a routine check-up?

During a routine check-up, your dentist will examine your teeth and gums for signs of decay, cavities, gum disease, and other oral health issues. They may also take X-rays and perform a cleaning to remove any plaque or tartar buildup.

Do I need to bring anything with me to my appointment?

Yes, it's a good idea to bring your insurance card, photo ID, and any necessary payment information. You may also want to bring a list of any medications you are taking or any concerns you have about your oral health.

What if I have dental anxiety or fear of the dentist?

It's important to let your dentist know if you have dental anxiety or fear of the dentist. They can work with you to help make your visit more comfortable, such as offering sedation options or taking breaks during longer procedures.

How can I improve my oral health?

Maintaining good oral hygiene habits, such as brushing twice a day and flossing daily, and following a healthy diet can help improve your oral health. Regular dental check-ups and cleanings are also important for preventing and treating dental problems. Your dentist can provide personalized recommendations based on your individual needs.

How long will my appointment take?

The length of your appointment will depend on the reason for your visit. A routine check-up and cleaning typically takes about an hour, while more complex procedures may take longer. Your dentist can provide an estimate of how long your appointment will take.
